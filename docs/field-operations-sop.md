# Field Operations SOP

This SOP translates the guest operations escalation runbook into onsite execution standards for property quality, maintenance coordination, and vendor follow-through.

It should be used by duty managers, local runners, cleaning partners, maintenance vendors, and any operator responsible for restoring guest-ready conditions in the field.

## Operating Standard

- Protect guest safety first, then access, then habitability, then presentation quality.
- Dispatch only after basic remote triage is complete unless the incident is safety-critical.
- Every field task must have a named owner, ETA, arrival confirmation, and completion proof.
- Vendors and runners must report facts, not assumptions, with timestamped photos and clear next actions.
- If the issue requires added headcount, new vendor coverage, or non-routine spend, escalate to the COO or CEO instead of improvising.

## Response Tiers

| Tier | Typical Triggers | Dispatch Rule | Arrival Target | Completion Target |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Tier 1 | Lockout, access failure, missing key, urgent guest assist | Local runner or lockout support if remote recovery fails | 30 min | Same visit where possible |
| Tier 2 | Cleaning failure, missing linen, consumables, minor reset tasks | Cleaning partner or runner once issue is visually confirmed | 60 min | 2 hours |
| Tier 3 | No hot water, HVAC outage, appliance failure, Wi-Fi hardware issue | Maintenance vendor after remote troubleshooting | 90 min urgent / same day routine | 2 hours workaround, full repair per scope |
| Tier 4 | Leak, electrical smell, fire concern, gas concern, unsafe condition | Emergency vendor and building contact immediately | Immediate dispatch | Emergency stabilization first |

## Dispatch Workflow

1. Confirm reservation, property, unit, and exact issue location.
2. Review remote triage already completed by guest operations.
3. Choose dispatch type: runner, cleaning, maintenance, locksmith, security, or emergency response.
4. Assign a single incident owner responsible for updates until closure.
5. Send vendor brief with:
   - property and unit
   - access method
   - guest impact
   - required arrival SLA
   - photo and reporting requirements
6. Confirm ETA back to guest operations within 5 minutes of dispatch.
7. Track arrival, onsite findings, work performed, and whether follow-up is still required.

## Field Checklists By Scenario

### 1. Lockout or Access Failure

- Confirm whether failure is at building entry, lockbox, smart lock, or unit door.
- Attempt one remote recovery path before dispatching in-person support.
- Dispatch local runner or locksmith if access is not restored within 10 minutes.
- Carry or arrange backup access method: key, code reset, credential reissue, or lockbox swap.
- Verify guest entry before closing the incident.

### 2. Cleaning Failure at Check-In

- Request wide-angle and defect photos before dispatch when possible.
- Confirm whether unit is untouched, partially turned, or quality-deficient after cleaning.
- Dispatch reclean crew immediately for any bathroom, bed, kitchen, or floor standard failure.
- If full reclean will exceed 60 minutes, instruct guest operations to prepare delay compensation or luggage-hold options.
- Reinspect critical zones: entry, bathroom, kitchen, beds, linens, bins, and odor.

### 3. No Hot Water

- Confirm whether outage affects one fixture or the full unit.
- Check boiler reset path, breaker state, and known building outage status.
- Dispatch maintenance if no recovery within 15 minutes of remote triage.
- If repair will exceed 2 hours, provide workaround plan to guest operations.
- Escalate to COO if restoration is unlikely within 12 hours and relocation may be needed.

### 4. Heating or Air Conditioning Failure

- Record indoor temperature, outdoor weather, and occupied rooms affected.
- Verify thermostat mode, breaker state, filter access, and visible error codes.
- Dispatch HVAC support if remote recovery fails within 15 minutes.
- Deliver temporary solution if stocked locally: heater, fan, or approved portable unit.
- Escalate habitability risk to COO when temperature cannot be stabilized safely.

### 5. Wi-Fi Outage

- Confirm if issue is device-specific, router-specific, or building/provider-wide.
- Check router power, cabling, reboot status, and ISP outage reports.
- Dispatch runner only when onsite reset is required or spare hardware must be installed.
- Provide hotspot or alternate connectivity option for business-critical stays when available.
- Leave final note stating whether the fix was onsite, provider-side, or still pending.

### 6. Noise Complaint or Guest Conduct Issue

- Confirm source unit, time pattern, and whether threat or violence is alleged.
- Coordinate with guest operations before any in-person contact.
- Dispatch security or local field rep for repeat disturbance or failed verbal warning.
- Record observed conditions factually and avoid argumentative guest interaction.
- Escalate to CEO only for major safety, police, or reputational incidents.

### 7. Lost Key or Access Device

- Confirm whether the guest is currently secure inside or locked out.
- Disable compromised credential or re-secure lockbox immediately when applicable.
- Dispatch replacement key or fob delivery with chain-of-custody note.
- Record whether replacement fee conditions were disclosed before handoff.
- Verify old access method is no longer usable before closure.

### 8. Water Leak or Plumbing Emergency

- Instruct immediate safety action first: isolate water or clear the area if safe.
- Dispatch emergency plumber and notify building representative immediately.
- Capture source, spread, ceiling or floor damage, and adjacent-unit risk.
- Move guest out of danger zone and escalate relocation risk to guest operations.
- Escalate to CEO if insurer contact, major damage, or multi-unit impact is likely.

### 9. Appliance Failure

- Confirm the affected appliance and whether the stay is materially impaired.
- Attempt only approved basic resets; do not improvise repairs onsite.
- Dispatch repair for fridge, cooker, or washer failures that affect the active stay.
- Arrange workaround: cooler, alternate unit laundry, or replacement small appliance.
- Close only once workaround or repair outcome is clearly documented.

## Vendor Dispatch Standards

### Maintenance Vendors

- Urgent habitability incidents: confirm ETA within 10 minutes, arrive within 90 minutes.
- Critical hazards: accept immediately and mobilize without delay.
- Routine follow-up work: schedule same day when guest occupied, next business day otherwise.
- Must report diagnosis, temporary fix, permanent fix plan, parts needed, and return visit timing.

### Cleaning Partners

- Failed arrival clean: confirm ETA within 10 minutes, arrive within 60 minutes.
- Mid-stay quality recovery: same-shift attendance unless issue is minor and guest agrees otherwise.
- Must report room-by-room completion with photos of kitchen, bathroom, beds, and floors.

### Lockout Support / Local Runners

- Lockout or access task: confirm ETA within 5 minutes, arrive within 30 minutes.
- Supply drop or minor reset: arrive within 60 minutes unless guest-impacting urgency requires faster service.
- Must report exact handoff result: guest entered, code changed, key exchanged, or follow-up required.

### Security Support

- Active disturbance: confirm dispatch within 5 minutes, arrive within 30 minutes.
- Must report whether warning was issued, parties separated, or public authorities contacted.

## Required Proof of Completion

Every field incident must include:

- arrival timestamp
- departure timestamp
- named responder or vendor company
- root cause or observed condition
- actions performed onsite
- guest-impact status after work
- next action, if not fully resolved

Photo standards:

- Access issues: entry point, lockbox or lock hardware, and successful access proof when appropriate
- Cleaning issues: before and after photos for each affected room
- Maintenance issues: source of failure, repaired component, and restored condition
- Leak or damage events: origin, spread, affected surfaces, and mitigation actions
- Supply drops or replacements: delivered item in place and ready for guest use

## Escalation Rules

- Escalate to COO for staffing shortages, uncovered shifts, missing runner zones, repeat vendor SLA failures, or any relocation decision.
- Escalate to CEO for major safety incidents, insurer-level property damage, legal exposure, or brand-threatening guest incidents.
- Do not commit to refunds, relocation, or non-standard compensation from the field team.

## Coverage Gaps Requiring COO Follow-Up

- No documented backup local runner roster is defined for simultaneous lockouts or after-hours overlaps.
- No named backup locksmith, plumber, or HVAC vendor coverage matrix is documented by zone.
- No spare-device inventory standard is defined for routers, lock batteries, key safes, or temporary comfort equipment.
- No formal field inspection checklist is linked to turnover QA for pre-arrival prevention.
- No on-call weekend security coverage standard is documented for repeat noise or conduct incidents.

## Minimum Audit Trail

Before closing any field incident, the owner must verify:

- guest operations received the final ETA and completion update
- photos and notes are attached
- unresolved follow-up work has a named owner and deadline
- any staffing or vendor gap has been escalated to the COO or CEO when required
