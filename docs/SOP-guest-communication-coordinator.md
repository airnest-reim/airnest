# SOP — Guest Communication Coordinator
**Role:** Liaz | **Hours:** Wednesday–Sunday, 9:00–00:00 | **Language:** English

---

## Team & Escalation Chain

| Person | Role | When to contact |
|--------|------|-----------------|
| **Liaz** | Guest Comm Coordinator | First point of contact for all guest issues |
| **Kim** | Property Manager | Property/quality issues requiring on-site action or decisions beyond Liaz's scope |
| **Cleaning team leads** | Cleaning execution | Via WhatsApp group (Liaz is member) — for cleaning & linen issues |
| **Jazz** | Back Office | Invoices, refunds to process, booking admin, platform disputes |
| **Corentin** | COO | Physical safety, legal risk, major refund decision, strategy only |

**Rule:** Corentin is never contacted for day-to-day issues. If in doubt, contact Kim first.

---

## Communication Standards

- **Channels (in order):** WhatsApp → PMS messaging (Airbnb/Booking) → Phone call (last resort)
- **Response time:** < 5 min during working hours
- **Style:** Short, direct, action-focused. No long apologies. Acknowledge → act → confirm.
- **Lockbox codes:** Always check PMS before responding to any access issue
- **After midnight:** Urgent calls rotate to all team members — first to answer takes it

## SOP by Situation

---

### 1. Guest cannot find the lockbox / does not know how to open it

- **Severity:** High
- **First response:** < 3 min
- **Resolution:** < 10 min
- **Owner:** Liaz
- **Steps:**
  1. Open PMS → find the property → locate lockbox code and exact location description
  2. Send guest the lockbox exact location (floor, side of door, building entrance, etc.) + code
  3. If no answer within 5 min, call the guest directly
  4. Confirm guest is inside
- **Escalate to Kim if:** Guest is still blocked after 15 min

---

### 2. Lockbox code is not working

- **Severity:** High
- **First response:** < 3 min
- **Resolution:** < 20 min
- **Owner:** Liaz → Kim
- **Steps:**
  1. Verify the code in PMS — confirm it is correct
  2. Ask guest to retry: enter code slowly, press firmly, wait for green light
  3. If still not working: contact Kim immediately via WhatsApp
  4. Kim coordinates emergency access (spare key, physical intervention)
  5. Keep guest updated every 5 min with a new ETA
- **Escalate to Kim:** Immediately if code retry fails

---

### 3. Key or access badge lost by guest

- **Severity:** Medium
- **First response:** < 5 min
- **Resolution:** < 24 h
- **Owner:** Liaz → Kim
- **Steps:**
  1. Confirm guest still has access to the property (can they get in?)
  2. If locked out: treat as situation #2
  3. If still inside: inform Kim, who arranges key replacement
  4. Log the incident in PMS
  5. Replacement fee applies per policy — inform guest in writing
- **Escalate to Kim:** Immediately for key replacement logistics

---

### 4. Property not clean on arrival

- **Severity:** High
- **First response:** < 5 min
- **Resolution:** < 2 h (reclean dispatched)
- **Owner:** Liaz → Cleaning team → Kim if unresolved
- **Steps:**
  1. Apologize briefly, ask guest to send 2–3 photos
  2. Message the cleaning WhatsApp group with property name, photos, and urgency
  3. Confirm ETA directly with the cleaning team lead who responds
  4. Give guest that ETA — do not promise under 1 h without cleaner confirmation
  5. If no cleaner responds within 15 min: escalate to Kim
  6. If guest refuses to wait: escalate to Kim for relocation or compensation decision
- **Escalate to Kim:** If cleaning team doesn't respond in 15 min, or if guest demands relocation/compensation

---

### 5. Missing or insufficient linen / towels

- **Severity:** Medium
- **First response:** < 5 min
- **Resolution:** < 2 h
- **Owner:** Liaz → Cleaning team → Kim if unresolved
- **Steps:**
  1. Confirm exactly what is missing (sheets, towels, pillowcases, quantity)
  2. Message the cleaning WhatsApp group with property name and what is needed
  3. Confirm delivery ETA directly with the cleaner who responds
  4. Give guest that ETA
  5. Follow up once delivery is confirmed
- **Escalate to Kim:** If cleaning team doesn't respond in 15 min

---

### 6. WiFi not working

- **Severity:** Medium
- **First response:** < 5 min
- **Resolution:** < 30 min
- **Owner:** Liaz (then Kim if on-site needed)
- **Steps:**
  1. Ask guest: is the router powered on? (lights visible?)
  2. Ask guest to unplug the router, wait 30 seconds, plug back in
  3. Wait 2 min then ask guest to reconnect
  4. If still not working: check PMS for network name and password, resend to guest
  5. If issue persists after reboot: contact Kim for on-site check or ISP call
- **Escalate to Kim if:** Not resolved within 20 min

---

### 7. Heating or air conditioning not working

- **Severity:** High
- **First response:** < 5 min
- **Resolution:** < 2 h (workaround) / < 24 h (repair)
- **Owner:** Liaz → Kim
- **Steps:**
  1. Ask: is the thermostat on? What temperature is set?
  2. Guide guest through basic reset (thermostat off/on, check breaker)
  3. If no improvement: contact Kim immediately
  4. Kim dispatches maintenance
  5. If not habitable (below 16°C / above 30°C at night): escalate to Corentin for relocation decision
- **Escalate to Kim:** Within 10 min if remote fix fails
- **Escalate to Corentin:** If property is uninhabitable

---

### 8. No hot water

- **Severity:** High
- **First response:** < 5 min
- **Resolution:** < 2 h
- **Owner:** Liaz → Kim
- **Steps:**
  1. Ask: is there any warm water or completely cold?
  2. Check if it is a building-wide outage (ask if neighbors affected)
  3. Contact Kim immediately — he contacts maintenance
  4. If repair exceeds 12 h: Corentin decides on compensation or relocation
- **Escalate to Kim:** Immediately

---

### 9. Appliance failure (fridge, washing machine, dishwasher, oven)

- **Severity:** Medium
- **First response:** < 10 min
- **Resolution:** < 24 h
- **Owner:** Liaz → Kim
- **Steps:**
  1. Identify the appliance and the impact on the stay
  2. Provide a workaround if possible (nearest laundromat, supermarket nearby)
  3. Contact Kim — he assesses urgency and dispatches repair
  4. For long stays (3+ nights) with broken fridge: Corentin approves compensation
- **Escalate to Kim:** Any appliance requiring on-site repair

---

### 10. Plumbing issue / water leak

- **Severity:** Critical
- **First response:** < 2 min
- **Resolution:** Immediate dispatch
- **Owner:** Liaz → Kim → Corentin
- **Steps:**
  1. If active leak: tell guest to close the water shut-off valve if accessible and safe
  2. Contact Kim immediately
  3. If Kim unreachable: contact Corentin directly
  4. Kim dispatches emergency plumber
  5. Assess if guest must vacate — Corentin makes that call
- **Escalate to Corentin:** Any active leak, flooding, or risk of property damage

---

### 11. Electrical issue (no power, burning smell, sparks)

- **Severity:** Critical
- **First response:** < 2 min
- **Resolution:** Immediate dispatch
- **Owner:** Liaz → Corentin
- **Steps:**
  1. If burning smell or sparks: tell guest to leave the property immediately and call emergency services
  2. Contact Corentin directly
  3. If only partial power loss: ask guest to check the circuit breaker (location in PMS)
  4. If breaker reset does not work: contact Kim for maintenance dispatch
- **Escalate to Corentin:** Any safety risk, burning smell, sparks, or full power outage

---

### 12. Noise complaint — guest is the source (neighbor complaint)

- **Severity:** High
- **First response:** < 5 min
- **Resolution:** < 15 min
- **Owner:** Liaz → Kim
- **Steps:**
  1. Contact guest on WhatsApp: politely inform them of the complaint, reference house rules
  2. Document the warning in PMS
  3. If complaint repeats within the same night: contact Kim
  4. Kim decides on further action (second warning, early checkout without refund)
- **Escalate to Kim:** On second complaint or if guest is aggressive

---

### 13. Noise complaint — guest is the victim (noisy neighbors)

- **Severity:** Medium–High
- **First response:** < 5 min
- **Resolution:** < 30 min
- **Owner:** Liaz → Kim
- **Steps:**
  1. Acknowledge and empathize briefly
  2. If noise is from another Airnest unit: contact Kim who handles the noisy party
  3. If noise is from outside (neighbor, street): advise guest, offer earplugs if available in property
  4. If unbearable and persists after midnight: contact Kim for possible relocation assessment
- **Escalate to Kim:** If guest requests relocation or compensation

---

### 14. Late checkout request

- **Severity:** Low
- **First response:** < 10 min
- **Resolution:** Same conversation
- **Owner:** Liaz
- **Steps:**
  1. Check PMS: is there an incoming guest same day? What time is the cleaning scheduled?
  2. If no conflict: approve up to 11:00 without asking anyone
  3. If guest wants 12:00 or later: check with Kim before confirming
  4. Confirm in writing with the approved time
- **Escalate to Kim:** Requests beyond 11:00 or if cleaning schedule unclear

---

### 15. Early check-in request

- **Severity:** Low
- **First response:** < 10 min
- **Resolution:** Same conversation
- **Owner:** Liaz → Kim
- **Steps:**
  1. Check PMS: when did the previous guest check out? Is cleaning complete?
  2. If property is confirmed ready: approve and give access
  3. If cleaning is in progress: give realistic ETA and offer luggage storage guidance
  4. Never promise early check-in without confirming with Kim
- **Escalate to Kim:** Any time you are unsure if the property is ready

---

### 16. Guest requests refund or compensation

- **Severity:** Medium–High
- **First response:** < 15 min
- **Resolution:** < 24 h
- **Owner:** Liaz → Jazz → Corentin
- **Steps:**
  1. Acknowledge the issue without admitting fault or promising a refund
  2. Log the request in PMS with full details (issue, amount requested, dates)
  3. Contact Jazz with the PMS record — she handles admin processing
  4. Jazz assesses against refund policy and processes if within standard thresholds
  5. Amounts above policy threshold or contested cases go to Corentin
- **Never promise a refund without Jazz or Corentin approval**
- **Escalate to Corentin:** Amounts above policy threshold, PR risk, or platform dispute

---

### 17. Guest threatens a bad review

- **Severity:** High
- **First response:** < 5 min
- **Resolution:** < 1 h
- **Owner:** Liaz → Kim / Jazz → Corentin
- **Steps:**
  1. Do not argue or get defensive. Acknowledge their frustration.
  2. Focus on solving the underlying issue first — a resolved problem rarely becomes a bad review
  3. If issue is operational (cleaning, access, maintenance): loop in Kim immediately
  4. If issue is financial (refund, compensation): loop in Jazz
  5. If guest is threatening publicly or the situation is PR-sensitive: contact Corentin
- **Escalate to Corentin:** Any public threat, social media mention, or situation with reputational risk

---

### 18. Safety emergency (fire, gas leak, injury)

- **Severity:** Critical
- **First response:** < 1 min
- **Resolution:** Immediate
- **Owner:** Liaz → Corentin (simultaneously)
- **Steps:**
  1. Tell guest to evacuate immediately if fire or gas smell
  2. Tell guest to call emergency services (112 in Europe)
  3. Contact Corentin immediately — he takes over
  4. Do not attempt to manage a safety emergency alone
- **Escalate to Corentin:** Immediately and without exception

---

### 19. Guest is lost / cannot find the property

- **Severity:** Medium
- **First response:** < 3 min
- **Resolution:** < 15 min
- **Owner:** Liaz
- **Steps:**
  1. Ask guest for their current location (street name or landmark)
  2. Open PMS → property address → send Google Maps pin on WhatsApp
  3. Give step-by-step directions from their location
  4. If building entrance is tricky: describe it exactly (color, street number, intercom)
  5. Stay on WhatsApp until guest confirms they are inside
- **Escalate to Kim:** If guest is still lost after 15 min (may need someone to meet them)

---

### 20. Parking issue

- **Severity:** Low–Medium
- **First response:** < 10 min
- **Resolution:** < 30 min
- **Owner:** Liaz → Kim
- **Steps:**
  1. Check PMS for parking instructions for that property
  2. Send guest the exact parking info (spot number, code, badge)
  3. If parking is full or access is blocked: contact Kim
  4. Kim coordinates with building management or finds alternative
- **Escalate to Kim:** Any parking situation requiring physical intervention

---

### 21. Guest complains it is too cold in the apartment

- **Severity:** Medium–High (depends on temperature)
- **First response:** < 5 min
- **Resolution:** < 1 h
- **Owner:** Liaz → Kim
- **Steps:**
  1. Check: is the heating on? What does the thermostat say?
  2. Guide guest to increase thermostat temperature
  3. Ask if radiators are warm to the touch (rule out a heating failure)
  4. If heating is working but apartment is still cold (old building, poor insulation): contact Kim for extra blankets or portable heater
  5. If heating is not working at all: treat as situation #7
- **Escalate to Kim:** If guest is uncomfortable after thermostat adjustment

---

### 22. Equipment missing that was listed in the listing

- **Severity:** Medium
- **First response:** < 10 min
- **Resolution:** < 24 h
- **Owner:** Liaz → Kim
- **Steps:**
  1. Check PMS listing to confirm the item was indeed advertised
  2. If confirmed: apologize, contact Kim immediately to arrange delivery or replacement
  3. If not confirmed in listing: politely clarify with guest — do not promise delivery
  4. If the missing item significantly impacts the stay (baby cot, essential appliance): Kim prioritizes same-day
  5. Log in PMS
- **Escalate to Kim:** Any missing item requiring physical delivery or sourcing
